# Your First Steps

You've created your account, subscribed, and connected your integrations. Here's how to make the most of ErkeScan from day one.

## 1. Explore the Screener

The [Real-Time Screener](../features/screener.md) is your home base. Open it and spend a few minutes getting familiar with the layout:

- **Sort columns** by clicking any header — try sorting by "OI Change 1h" to find coins with the biggest open interest shifts
- **Star your favorites** — click the star icon next to any coin to add it to your favorites list for quick access
- **Click any coin** to open its detailed chart modal with price, volume, OI, and more

## 2. Check the Signals Tab

Open the **Signals** tab in the web app to see what the momentum engine is detecting right now:

- Look for **Grade A** and **Grade B** signals — these are the strongest setups
- Signals marked **HC** (High Confidence) have the highest probability
- Note the **direction** — LONG or SHORT — and use it alongside your own analysis

## 3. Create Your First Alert

Go to **Alerts** and try creating a simple alert:

- Choose a metric (e.g., "OI Change 15m")
- Set a condition (e.g., "greater than 5%")
- Save the alert

When any coin meets your condition, you'll receive a Telegram notification instantly via [@erkescanalert\_bot](https://t.me/erkescanalert_bot). See [Custom Alerts](../features/custom-alerts.md) for advanced alert strategies.

## 4. Analyze with Charts

Pick any coin and open [Charts & Analytics](../features/charts-and-analytics.md) to dig deeper:

- Check the **funding rate** chart to see if the market is overleveraged
- Review **open interest** trends alongside price to confirm moves
- Use **CVD (Cumulative Volume Delta)** to see real buy/sell pressure

## Tips for New Users

- **Start simple**: create 2-3 basic alerts and observe how they trigger before building complex rule sets
- **Use multiple timeframes**: the screener shows data across 5m, 15m, 1h, 8h, and 1d — use longer timeframes for trend direction and shorter ones for entries
- **Combine tools**: use the Signals tab for ideas, the Screener to validate, and Charts to analyze
