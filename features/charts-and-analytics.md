# Charts & Analytics

ErkeScan provides 16 types of interactive charts that let you analyze any coin from every angle. Whether you're building a trade thesis, confirming a signal, or reviewing historical performance — the charts section has the data you need.

## Available Chart Types

### Price & Performance

**Price Chart** — Standard price chart for any tracked futures pair. Useful as a baseline for all other analysis.

**Cumulative Return Chart** — Shows total percentage return over a selected period. Great for comparing how a coin has performed relative to your entry or over a specific timeframe.

**Return by Day / Hour** — Breaks down performance by day of the week or hour of the day. Helps identify if a coin tends to be more active during certain sessions (e.g., Asia vs US hours).

### Volume Analysis

**Volume Chart (24h)** — Daily volume bars showing trading activity over time. Useful for spotting volume trends — increasing volume in an uptrend confirms strength.

**Volume Chart (Intraday)** — Granular volume data within a single day. Helps identify specific time windows with unusual activity.

**CVD (Cumulative Volume Delta) Dollar Chart** — Shows the running total of buy volume minus sell volume in dollar terms. Rising CVD = persistent buying pressure. Falling CVD = persistent selling. This is one of the most powerful indicators for detecting real demand vs supply.

### Open Interest

**Open Interest Chart** — Total open interest over time. Rising OI means new positions are being opened; falling OI means positions are being closed.

**OI Changes & Price Changes** — Overlays OI changes with price changes to reveal the relationship between positioning and price action. The four combinations (OI up/price up, OI up/price down, OI down/price up, OI down/price down) each tell a different story.

**OI Snapshots** — Point-in-time snapshots of open interest across the market, useful for comparing relative positioning across coins.

### Funding & Leverage

**Funding Rate Chart** — Historical funding rates over time. Helps you see how long a funding extreme has persisted and when it tends to revert. Sustained high positive funding often precedes a long squeeze.

**Annualized Basis Chart** — The annualized premium of futures price over spot price. A high basis means traders are willing to pay a premium for leverage — it reflects overall market optimism (or pessimism when negative).

### Liquidations

**Liquidations Chart** — Shows the dollar value of liquidated positions over time. Large liquidation events create cascading price moves and are often the catalyst for dramatic wicks.

### Sector & Comparison

**Sector Performance Chart** — Groups coins by sector (L1, L2, DeFi, Meme, AI, Gaming, etc.) and shows how each sector is performing. Useful for identifying sector rotation — when money flows from one sector to another.

**Spaghetti Charts** — Multi-line chart that overlays 4–10 coins on the same chart for direct comparison. See [Spaghetti Charts](spaghetti-charts.md) for details.

### Whale Activity

**Whale Trades Chart** — Visual representation of large transactions over time, correlated with price action.

## How to Use Charts

1. Navigate to **Charts** in the sidebar
2. Select a coin from the dropdown or search bar
3. Choose a chart type
4. Adjust the timeframe as needed
5. Hover over data points for detailed values

All charts are interactive — you can zoom, pan, and hover for precise values.

## Combining Charts for Analysis

The most effective way to use charts is in combination. Here's an example workflow:

1. Start with the **Price Chart** to understand the current trend
2. Check the **OI Chart** — is OI rising or falling with the price move?
3. Look at **CVD** — is there genuine buying/selling pressure behind the move?
4. Check the **Funding Rate** — is the market overleveraged in one direction?
5. Review **Liquidations** — have there been recent forced closures that might trigger further moves?

This multi-chart approach gives you a complete picture before entering any position.
