# Signal Scanner

The Signal Scanner is ErkeScan's proprietary momentum detection engine. It continuously analyzes every tracked futures pair and identifies coins showing strong directional momentum — giving you a clear, graded view of the best setups in the market right now.

## How It Works

The Signal Scanner evaluates each coin across 9 key factors and produces a composite grade. Unlike simple alerts that trigger on a single condition, the Signal Scanner looks at the full picture of what's happening with a coin before flagging it.

## Signal Grades

Every detected signal receives a letter grade from **A** (strongest) to **F** (weakest):

| Grade | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **A** | Exceptional setup — multiple strong factors aligned |
| **B** | Strong setup — most factors confirm the direction |
| **C** | Moderate setup — some factors support, some are neutral |
| **D** | Weak setup — mixed signals, proceed with caution |
| **F** | Minimal edge — most factors are neutral or conflicting |

## High Confidence (HC) Badge

Signals that meet an especially high threshold across multiple factors receive the **HC (High Confidence)** badge. These represent the setups where the scanner has the strongest conviction. HC signals are relatively rare — when they appear, they deserve your attention.

## Signal Direction

Each signal shows a clear direction:

- **LONG** — the scanner detects upward momentum
- **SHORT** — the scanner detects downward momentum
- **WATCH** — activity is noteworthy but direction isn't yet clear

## The 9 Factors

The Signal Scanner evaluates each coin across these dimensions:

1. **Momentum** — Is the price accelerating in one direction?
2. **Trapped Liquidity** — Are there positions likely to be forced closed, adding fuel to the move?
3. **Open Interest Flow** — Is new money flowing in or out, and does it confirm the price direction?
4. **Acceleration** — Is the rate of change increasing (move getting faster)?
5. **Idiosyncratic Move** — Is this coin moving independently from the broader market (not just following BTC)?
6. **Order Flow** — What does the buy/sell pressure balance look like?
7. **Overextension** — Is the move already stretched too far, reducing the probability of continuation?
8. **Funding Alignment** — Does the funding rate support or contradict the move?
9. **Multi-Period Confluence** — Do multiple timeframes agree on the direction?

Each factor can contribute positively, negatively, or neutrally to the overall grade.

## Market Regime

The scanner also identifies the broader market regime:

| Regime | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Euphoria** | Extreme bullish sentiment — caution for long entries |
| **Bull / Bullish** | Healthy uptrend — favor long setups |
| **Neutral** | No clear direction — be selective |
| **Bearish / Bear** | Downtrend — favor short setups |
| **Fear** | Extreme bearish sentiment — caution for short entries |
| **Transition** | Regime is changing — wait for confirmation |

## Win Rate Tracking

The Signal Scanner tracks historical performance with a calibration factor, showing you the percentage of past signals at each grade that resulted in a profitable move. This helps you understand the reliability of different grade levels over time.

## How to Use Signals

**As a starting point, not a final answer.** Signals tell you where to look — always do your own analysis before entering a trade. Open the coin's charts, check the screener data, and make sure the setup aligns with your trading plan.

**Focus on A and B grades.** Lower-grade signals have lower hit rates. If you're selective with entries, concentrate on the strongest setups.

**Pay attention to direction flips.** When a coin's signal flips from LONG to SHORT (or vice versa), it often indicates a meaningful shift in market structure.

**Use regime context.** A LONG signal during a Bull regime has better odds than a LONG signal during a Bear regime. Let the broader environment guide your position sizing and conviction.
