# Spaghetti Charts

Spaghetti Charts are a multi-line visualization tool that lets you overlay 4 to 10 different coins on the same chart for direct comparison. This makes it easy to spot which assets are leading, lagging, diverging, or moving in sync.

## What They Show

Each selected coin gets its own colored line on the chart, normalized so you can compare percentage performance on an equal footing regardless of the coin's absolute price. This means you can compare BTC's 2% move directly against a small-cap altcoin's 8% move on the same scale.

## How to Use

1. Navigate to **Charts** and select **Spaghetti Charts**
2. Add 4–10 coins you want to compare
3. Choose your timeframe
4. The chart renders all selected coins as overlapping lines

### Interactive Features

- **Hover** over any point to see exact values for each coin at that timestamp
- **Click** a coin in the legend to isolate it (dims all other lines)
- **Double-click** the legend to restore all lines

## Use Cases

**Sector rotation detection** — Add all major L1 coins (BTC, ETH, SOL, AVAX, etc.) and watch for divergence. When one coin starts outperforming while others flatten or drop, it may indicate capital rotation.

**Relative strength analysis** — Compare a coin you're interested in against BTC and ETH. If your coin is consistently outperforming both during dips and rallies, it shows relative strength — a bullish signal.

**Pair trading ideas** — Find two historically correlated coins. When they diverge significantly, the gap often closes — creating a pair trade opportunity (long the laggard, short the leader).

**Sector comparisons** — Compare representatives from different sectors (e.g., an L1 vs a DeFi token vs an AI token) to see where money is flowing.

## Tips

- Keep charts to 4–6 coins for clarity. More than 8 lines gets visually noisy.
- Compare coins within the same sector first, then across sectors.
- Use longer timeframes (1d+) for macro comparisons and shorter timeframes for intraday rotation.
